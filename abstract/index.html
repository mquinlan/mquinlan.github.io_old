Original proposal

Essential question:

What is the history of the canonical Gospels and how does their history affect their significance in religious and secular contexts?

I wish to explore the canonical Gospels as both religious and historical writings - how each Gospel has influenced the figure of Jesus, the Christian Church and non-Christian history. I plan on using Professor Luke Timothy Johnson's 36 lecture course entitled 'Jesus and the Gospels,' which "approaches the Gospels and our perceptions of Jesus from a different perspective than the popular quest for the "historical Jesus."" I also hope to follow Professor Dale B. Martin's book and corresponding 'Open Yale Course' on 'New Testament History.' (I will be using the Oxford Annotated Bible for all readings.)
